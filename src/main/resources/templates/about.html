What is MBTI?

The Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) is one of the world's most widely used personality frameworks. Developed by Isabel Briggs Myers and her mother Katharine Cook Briggs, it's based on Carl Jung's theory of psychological types and categorizes people into 16 distinct personality types using four key dimensions.

E
/
I
Extraversion vs. Introversion

How do you recharge your energy? Extraverts gain energy from social interactions and external stimulation. Introverts recharge through solitude, reflection, and inner focus.

S
/
N
Sensing vs. Intuition

How do you take in information? Sensors focus on concrete facts, details, and present reality. Intuitives see patterns, possibilities, and future potential.

T
/
F
Thinking vs. Feeling

How do you make decisions? Thinkers rely on logic, objective analysis, and principles. Feelers prioritize values, empathy, and the impact on people.

J
/
P
Judging vs. Perceiving

How do you approach life? Judgers prefer structure, planning, and decisiveness. Perceivers embrace flexibility, spontaneity, and keeping options open.

💡 Part 2

Why Does Personality Matter?

Understanding your personality type is not just a fun exercise — it's a powerful tool for self-awareness, better relationships, and intentional growth.

🪞

Self-Awareness First

Knowing your personality type helps you understand why you think, feel, and behave the way you do. It removes self-judgment and replaces it with understanding — you're not "broken," you're wired differently.

🤝

Better Relationships

When you understand your own type and learn about others', communication becomes easier. You gain empathy for people who process the world differently — reducing conflict at work, at home, and in friendships.

🎯

Smarter Career Choices

Different types thrive in different environments. Knowing your strengths and preferences helps you pick roles where you'll naturally excel — rather than spending years in a mismatch.

🌱

Targeted Self-Growth

Generic advice rarely sticks because it ignores who you are. Personality-based growth focuses your energy on the specific areas where improvement will have the biggest impact on your life.

The Big Five Personality Traits

While MBTI gives you a type, the Big Five (OCEAN) model — backed by decades of scientific research — measures your personality across five continuous dimensions. Psyche uses these traits to build your personalized habit plan.

🌌
Openness
Openness to Experience
O in OCEAN

Openness measures your curiosity, creativity, and willingness to explore new ideas, experiences, and perspectives. It's the trait of the artist, the philosopher, the innovator — people who love learning for its own sake and are drawn to novelty.

✦ High Openness
  • Imaginative & creative
  • Loves learning new things
  • Comfortable with ambiguity
  • Open to abstract ideas
  • Appreciates art & beauty
↓ Low Openness
  • Prefers routine & familiarity
  • Practical over theoretical
  • Conventional & traditional
  • Focused and detail-oriented
  • Prefers clear-cut answers
How Psyche helps you grow Openness
  • Daily micro-challenges to try something new
  • Journaling prompts for creative reflection
  • Curated reading & learning habits
  • Exposure exercises to expand your comfort zone
📋
Conscientiousness
Self-Discipline & Goal-Orientation
C in OCEAN

Conscientiousness is your capacity for organization, discipline, and follow-through. Highly conscientious people set goals and actually reach them. They're reliable, thorough, and strategic. This trait is one of the strongest predictors of success in academics, career, and health.

✦ High Conscientiousness
  • Organized & plans ahead
  • Reliable and punctual
  • Strong work ethic
  • Goal-directed behavior
  • High self-discipline
↓ Low Conscientiousness
  • Flexible & spontaneous
  • May procrastinate
  • Struggles with routine
  • Easy-going attitude
  • Dislikes rigid structure
How Psyche helps you grow Conscientiousness
  • Daily habit checklists with streak tracking
  • Time-blocking and planning exercises
  • Small commitment → big consistency challenges
  • Progress visualization to reinforce follow-through
⚡
Extraversion
Social Energy & Assertiveness
E in OCEAN

Extraversion measures how much you draw energy from the outside world — people, activity, and stimulation. It includes assertiveness, enthusiasm, and a tendency to seek out social situations. This isn't about being loud; it's about where your energy flows.

✦ High Extraversion
  • Energized by social interaction
  • Assertive & talkative
  • Enjoys being the center
  • Action-oriented
  • Draws energy from groups
↓ Low Extraversion (Introversion)
  • Recharges in solitude
  • Prefers deep conversations
  • Thinks before speaking
  • Selective socializing
  • Comfortable being alone
How Psyche helps you develop Extraversion
  • Social initiation challenges (one conversation/day)
  • Assertiveness-building micro-tasks
  • Public speaking & expression exercises
  • Networking and connection-making habits
🫶
Agreeableness
Empathy, Cooperation & Trust
A in OCEAN

Agreeableness reflects your orientation toward others — your empathy, compassion, and willingness to cooperate. Highly agreeable people prioritize harmony and are attuned to others' emotions. It's strongly linked to relationship satisfaction and prosocial behavior.

✦ High Agreeableness
  • Warm & compassionate
  • Cooperative & helpful
  • Trusting of others
  • Conflict-avoidant
  • Empathetic listener
↓ Low Agreeableness
  • Competitive & skeptical
  • Direct, even blunt
  • Prioritizes self-interest
  • Challenges others openly
  • Harder to build rapport
How Psyche helps you grow Agreeableness
  • Daily gratitude and kindness practices
  • Active listening exercises
  • Perspective-taking reflection prompts
  • Conflict-resolution skill-building habits
🌊
Neuroticism
Emotional Reactivity & Stability
N in OCEAN

Neuroticism measures your tendency toward negative emotional states — anxiety, moodiness, irritability, and emotional reactivity. Unlike the other four traits where higher is generally better, lower Neuroticism = better outcomes. It's the one trait Psyche helps you actively reduce.

✦ Low Neuroticism (Goal)
  • Emotionally stable
  • Calm under pressure
  • Resilient to setbacks
  • Consistent mood
  • Less stress and anxiety
↑ High Neuroticism
  • Prone to anxiety & worry
  • Mood swings
  • Sensitive to criticism
  • Overthinks situations
  • Emotional exhaustion
How Psyche helps you reduce Neuroticism
  • Mindfulness & breathwork daily habits
  • Cognitive reframing exercises
  • Stress-release physical activity tasks
  • Sleep hygiene and recovery routines
